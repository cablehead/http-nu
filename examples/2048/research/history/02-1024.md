# 1024 -- the intermediate clone (late February 2014)

About **21 days** after *Threes!* launched, **Veewo Studio** (a small
Chinese studio, later known for the *Super Phantom Cat* series)
released **1024** on the iOS App Store. Free.

The App Store description, at one point, read literally:

> no need to pay for Threes

So this was not subtle. It was openly positioned as "Threes!, but free."

## What 1024 changed

Two changes, both consequential:

1. **Equal-pair merges all the way up.** *Threes!* requires a 1+2 to
   bootstrap and then equal pairs from 3 onward. *1024* dropped the
   asymmetric step: every merge is `n + n = 2n`. The target is the 1024
   tile, hence the name. This is the rule 2048 inherited.
2. **Tiles slide across the whole board in the swipe direction**, not
   one cell. Also inherited by 2048.

Together these two changes are what the *Threes!* team meant when they
later called the descendants "broken" -- the constraints that made
*Threes!* a tight puzzle were gone, and a much looser, more forgiving
game took their place.

Veewo did not see themselves as bad actors at the time. Per later
coverage, they understood *1024* as "a test of sorts -- an attempt to
play with the *Threes!* formula and find out if they could improve on
it." The framing inside the studio was iteration; the framing from
outside was a free knockoff that arrived three weeks after the paid
original.

## The Cirulli connection

This part is worth pinning down because the public story sometimes
elides it. In **February 2014**, Gabriele Cirulli -- who had not yet
made 2048 -- **emailed Veewo** asking if he could base a school
assignment on *1024*. Veewo agreed and let him study the *1024*
codebase. He then went on to build 2048 over a weekend in early March,
crediting *1024* as the immediate inspiration.

So Cirulli's chain of acknowledgement was *2048 -> 1024*. *Threes!*
itself he did not initially know about; that's something he came to
later, and addressed in the Medium post covered in
[03-2048-launch.md](03-2048-launch.md).
