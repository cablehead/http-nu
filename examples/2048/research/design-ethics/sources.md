# Sources

## Primary statements

- **Jonathan Blow, *PC Gamer* interview** (2012): "Do you believe
  social games are evil? 'Yes. Absolutely.'" The canonical
  short-form statement of the "indictment" pole. Contains the
  "predicated on a kind of player exploitation," "Skinnerian
  reward schedules," and "waste their lives in front of our
  games" formulations.
  <https://www.pcgamer.com/jonathan-blow-interview-social-game-designers-goal-is-to-degrade-the-players-quality-of-life/>

- **Jonathan Blow, Creative Mornings PDX talk** (2010): "Game
  design: the medium is the message." Longer-form version of the
  same argument.
  <https://creativemornings.com/talks/jonathan-blow>

- **Jonathan Blow, "Shifting Intention"** (*Escapist* feature on
  Blow's design philosophy).
  <https://www.escapistmagazine.com/jonathan-blows-shifting-intention/>

- **Frank Lantz, *The Beauty of Games*** (MIT Press, 2024). The
  extended argument for the "craft" pole. Includes the slot-machine
  discussion and the engagement-vs-depth distinction.
  <https://mitpress.mit.edu/9780262552950/the-beauty-of-games/>

- **Asher Vollmer and Greg Wohlwend, "Threemails"** -- already
  cited in the history bundle. The most articulate primary
  document on the "we built the addictive version and chose not to
  ship it" position.
  <https://asherv.com/threes/threemails/>

- **Ian Bogost, "The Curse of Cow Clicker"** -- the designer-as-
  satirist case study. Already cited in the incrementals bundle.
  <http://bogost.com/writing/blog/the_curse_of_cow_clicker/>

## Interviews and secondary coverage

- **Frank Lantz, on the Sean Carroll podcast** (Episode 52, June
  2019): "Frank Lantz on the Logic and Emotion of Games."
  Long-form conversation that covers the slot machines / depth /
  engagement triangle in spoken form.
  <https://www.preposterousuniverse.com/podcast/2019/06/24/episode-52-frank-lantz-on-the-logic-and-emotion-of-games/>

- **Frank Lantz, *Thought Economics* interview**: "A Conversation
  with One of the World's Foremost Game Designers, Frank Lantz."
  <https://thoughteconomics.com/frank-lantz/>

- **Frank Lantz on the Jim Rutt Show** (Episode 210, transcript):
  "Frank Lantz on the Beauty of Games." Useful structured
  walk-through of the book's arguments.
  <https://jimruttshow.blubrry.net/the-jim-rutt-show-transcripts/transcript-of-ep-210-frank-lantz-on-the-beauty-of-games/>

## Academic context (cited but not fetched)

- **Scott Rigby and Richard M. Ryan**, *Glued to Games* (Praeger,
  2011). Already in the psychology-bundle sources. The academic
  framework most useful for understanding the *Threes!* /
  Lantz position -- distinguishes between need-satisfying
  engagement (depth) and need-thwarting engagement (compulsion).

- **Bogost, "Persuasive Games" (MIT Press, 2007)**. Bogost's
  earlier book, the framework from which his Cow Clicker work
  descended. Not strictly required for this chapter, but the
  most rigorous statement of "games as arguments" available.
